Introduction

Join us on a captivating journey through Pakistan's rich cultural tapestry with Crossroads Adventure's 12-day Experience. Discover the historical wonders of Karachi, Hyderabad, and Sehwan, where ancient tombs, mosques, and fortresses unveil tales of bygone eras. Traverse the scenic landscapes of Sukkur and Khairpur, exploring majestic forts and sacred shrines along the Indus River. Immerse yourself in the architectural splendor of Bahawalpur's mosques and palaces before delving into Multan's Sufi heritage and Lahore's Mughal marvels. From the ancient ruins of Harrappa to the modern skyline of Islamabad, each destination offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With expert guides and comfortable accommodations, Crossroads Adventure ensures a seamless and unforgettable experience for international travelers seeking adventure and exploration. Join us on this journey to unlock the hidden treasures of Pakistan's diverse landscapes, heritage sites and widen the horizons or your understanding.

Highlights

Explore Ancient Civilizations

Uncover the mysteries of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization at Harrappa and Taxila, where archaeological wonders like the Harrappa ruins and Taxila Museum reveal insights into humanity's past.

Marvel at Architectural Splendor

From the majestic Lahore Fort and Badshahi Mosque to the intricate tile work of Wazir Khan Mosque and the opulent Faiz Mahal in Khairpur, immerse yourself in the architectural grandeur of Pakistan's historical landmarks.

Experience Cultural Diversity

Delve into the vibrant culture of Pakistan as you wander through bustling bazaars like Anarkali Bazaar, explore sacred shrines like Lal Shahbaz Qalandar's, and witness traditional craftsmanship at the Folk Heritage Museum.

Journey Through Scenic Landscapes

Traverse the diverse landscapes of Pakistan, from the coastal beauty of Karachi's Clifton Beach to the desert vistas of Derawar Fort in Bahawalpur, offering breathtaking views and unforgettable experiences.

Connect with Spiritual Heritage

Experience the spiritual resonance of Sufi shrines like Shah Abdul Latif's in Sehwan and Shah Rukn e Alam's in Multan, where centuries-old traditions and sacred rituals come alive, offering moments of reflection and transcendence.

Itinerary

Upon your international arrival at Karachi Airport, our representative will warmly welcome you. You'll then be transferred to your hotel in Karachi. After settling in, immerse yourself in the vibrant city with an afternoon Experience.

Quaid E Azam Mausoleum: Pay homage to the founder of Pakistan at this iconic mausoleum, also known as Jinnah Mausoleum. Built in 1971, it stands as a symbol of Karachi's historical significance and reverence for its founding father.

Defense Mosque: Explore this architectural marvel, a testament to Karachi's rich cultural heritage and religious diversity. Its construction reflects a blend of traditional and contemporary design elements.

Clifton Beach (Sea View): Enjoy the scenic beauty of the Arabian Sea at Clifton Beach, a popular leisure spot for locals and tourists alike. From camel rides to buggy rides, the beach offers a range of activities for visitors to enjoy.

Mohatta Palace Museum: Delve into the history of Karachi at Mohatta Palace Museum, known for its modern Muslim architecture. Built during the British Raj, it provides insights into the city's colonial past and cultural evolution.

Overnight stay at Mehran Hotel.

After breakfast, check out from the hotel and Venture on a journey to Hyderabad, a city steeped in history and heritage.

Chaukundi Tombs: Explore the intricately carved sandstone tombs dating back to the early Islamic period. These tombs serve as a testament to the artistic and architectural prowess of ancient craftsmen.

Banbhore: Step back in time as you visit Banbhore, the earliest Islamic city outside Arabia. Established around 711 A.D., it bears witness to the region's rich historical legacy and cultural exchange.

Makli Necropolis, Thatta: Marvel at the grandeur of Makli Necropolis, one of the largest funerary sites in the world. With tombs dating back to the 14th century, it offers a glimpse into the artistic and spiritual traditions of bygone eras.

Shahjehan Mosque, Thatta: Admire the architectural splendor of Shahjehan Mosque, a masterpiece attributed to the builder of the Taj Mahal. Its intricate design and craftsmanship reflect the zenith of Mughal architectural style.

Overnight at Mawada Inn hotel Hyderabad.

Drive to Sehwan City and en-route visit:

Shrine of Shah Abdul Latif, Bhit Shah: Explore the sacred shrine dedicated to Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai, the renowned Sindhi poet and Sufi saint. Built in 1772, it continues to draw devotees and visitors from around the world.

Ranikot Fort, Jamshoro: Witness the awe-inspiring grandeur of Ranikot Fort, also known as "The Great Wall of Sindh." Believed to be the world's largest fort, its origins date back to ancient times, serving as a symbol of power and defense.

Overnight stay at Sehwan Divine Hotel.

Venture on a scenic drive to Sukkur and en-route visit:

Shrine of Lal Shahbaz Qalandar: Pay homage to Lal Shahbaz Qalandar, a revered Sufi saint whose shrine attracts pilgrims and devotees throughout the year. The shrine stands as a symbol of spiritual unity and cultural harmony.

Moen Jo Daro, Larkana: Explore the ancient ruins of Moen Jo Daro, a UNESCO World Heritage Site dating back to 2500 BCE. As one of the earliest planned cities, it offers insights into the urban civilization of the ancient Indus Valley.

Overnight at the hotel in Sukkur.

Delve into Sukkur's architectural wonders:

Minaret of Masoom Shah: Admire the architectural elegance of Minaret of Masoom Shah, a historical landmark that reflects the region's rich cultural heritage.

Sat Behan Asthan, Rohri: Explore the historic resting place known for its exquisite blue tiles and Quranic inscriptions, offering a glimpse into the region's artistic and spiritual traditions.

Sukkur Barrage: Witness the engineering marvel of Sukkur Barrage, which has played a vital role in controlling the flow of the Indus River and supporting agricultural activities in the region.

Later, proceed to Khairpur and visit:

Faiz Mahal: Step into the opulent world of Faiz Mahal, a magnificent palace built in the 18th century by the rulers of Khairpur. Its intricate architecture and luxurious interiors reflect the grandeur of bygone eras.

Kot DG Fort: Explore the ancient fort of Kot DG, which stands as a symbol of the region's historical significance and architectural heritage.

Overnight at the hotel in Khairpur.

Drive to Bahawalpur with visits to the:

Bhong Mosque, Rahim Yar Khan: Marvel at the architectural splendor of Bhong Mosque, renowned for its intricate design and exquisite calligraphy. Built over several decades, it stands as a testament to the region's rich cultural heritage.

Shrine Uch Sharief: Explore the historic city of Uch Sharief, known for its collection of shrines dedicated to Muslim mystics from the 12-15th centuries. Adorned with extensive tile work, these shrines offer insights into the region's spiritual and architectural traditions.

Overnight at hotel One.

Venture on an excursion to Multan:

Derawar Fort, Bahawalpur: Explore the imposing fort of Derawar, dating back to the 9th century AD. Built by Rai Jajja Bhati and later rebuilt by Nawab Sadeq Muhammad, it stands as a symbol of strength and resilience amidst the desert landscape.

Shrine of Shah Rukn e Alam, Multan: Pay homage to the revered Sufi saint Shah Rukn e Alam at his mausoleum, an architectural marvel dating back to the 14th century. Its exquisite design reflects the fusion of Central Asian and Persian architectural styles.

Overnight at hotel One.

Journey to Lahore with a visit to the ancient site of:

Adventure Distance: Multan to Lahore (Approx. 350 km, 4-5 hours drive) Harrappa: Explore the ruins of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization at Harrappa. Dating back to the Bronze Age, these archaeological remains offer insights into one of the world's earliest urban societies.

Adventure Time: Multan to Harrappa (Approx. 4 hours)

Overnight at Best Western hotel.

Discover the cultural and historical treasures of Lahore:

Lahore Fort: Step into the Mughal era at Lahore Fort, also known as Shahi Qila or Royal Fort. With its origins dating back to the 11th century, it stands as a testament to the grandeur and architectural prowess of the Mughal Empire.

Adventure Time: Best Western hotel to Lahore Fort (Approx. 20 minutes) Badshahi Mosque: Admire the grandeur of Badshahi Mosque, a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Built by Emperor Aurangzeb in the 17th century, it is one of Lahore's most iconic landmarks and the second largest mosque in Pakistan.

Adventure Time: Lahore Fort to Badshahi Mosque (Approx. 5 minutes) Anarkali Bazaar: Immerse yourself in the bustling atmosphere of Anarkali Bazaar, one of the oldest and busiest markets in Lahore. Named after the legendary courtesan Anarkali, it offers a vibrant array of shops selling everything from traditional handicrafts to modern goods.

Adventure Time: Badshahi Mosque to Anarkali Bazaar (Approx. 15 minutes) Wazir Khan Mosque: Marvel at the beauty of Wazir Khan Mosque, known for its intricate tile work and colorful frescoes. Built in the 17th century by the Mughal governor Wazir Khan, it remains a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Adventure Time: Anarkali Bazaar to Wazir Khan Mosque (Approx. 10 minutes) Shalimar Garden: Wander through the lush greenery of Shalimar Garden, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. Designed to evoke the paradise described in Islamic scriptures, it is a testament to the Mughal love for gardens and aesthetics.

Adventure Time: Wazir Khan Mosque to Shalimar Garden (Approx. 30 minutes) Overnight at the hotel.

Drive to Islamabad with a stop at:

Adventure Distance: Lahore to Islamabad (Approx. 375 km, 4-5 hours drive) Rohtas Fort, Jehlum: Explore the imposing fort of Rohtas, built in the 16th century by Raja Todar Mal on the orders of Sher Shah Suri. With its massive defensive walls and monumental gateways, it stands as a symbol of strength and military prowess.

Adventure Time: Lahore to Rohtas Fort (Approx. 3.5 hours)

Overnight stay at the hotel.

Experience Rawalpindi & Islamabad:

Adventure Distance: Islamabad to Rawalpindi (Approx. 15 km, 30 minutes drive)

The Shah Faisal Mosque: Marvel at the architectural grandeur of Shah Faisal Mosque, one of the largest mosques in the world. Built in the 1980s, its modern design and vast prayer hall make it a striking landmark of Islamabad.

Adventure Time: Hotel to Shah Faisal Mosque (Approx. 30 minutes) Shakarparian viewpoint: Soak in panoramic views of Islamabad and its surroundings from Shakarparian viewpoint. Located in the lush green hills of Shakarparian, it offers a serene escape from the city hustle. Adventure Time: Shah Faisal Mosque to Shakarparian viewpoint (Approx. 20 minutes)

Pakistan Monument: Learn about Pakistan's history and culture at Pakistan Monument, a national symbol commemorating the country's independence. Its unique design represents the four provinces of Pakistan and the territories of Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan. Adventure Time: Shakarparian viewpoint to Pakistan Monument (Approx. 15 minutes)

Folk Heritage Museum: Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Pakistan at Folk Heritage Museum. Featuring exhibits on traditional crafts, music, and lifestyle, it offers a glimpse into the diverse cultural tapestry of the country.

Adventure Time: Pakistan Monument to Folk Heritage Museum (Approx. 10 minutes)

Afternoon visit to Taxila:

Adventure Distance: Islamabad to Taxila (Approx. 40 km, 1 hour drive) Sirkap: Explore the archaeological remains of Sirkap, an ancient city founded by the Greco-Bactrian king Demetrius around 180 BC. Its well-planned layout and architectural features provide insights into the urban life of ancient Taxila.

Adventure Time: Folk Heritage Museum to Sirkap (Approx. 1 hour) Taxila Museum: Discover a treasure trove of Gandharan art at Taxila Museum, home to a vast collection of artifacts dating from the 1st to the 7th centuries CE. From stone sculptures to stucco reliefs, the museum showcases the artistic achievements of ancient Taxila. Adventure Time: Sirkap to Taxila Museum (Approx. 10 minutes) Return to Islamabad for overnight stay.

Transfer to Islamabad International Airport for your onward journey. Depart with cherished memories of Pakistan's rich cultural heritage and historical wonders.
