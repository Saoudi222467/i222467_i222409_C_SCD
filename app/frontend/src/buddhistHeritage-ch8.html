Buddhist Heritage Expedition

14 Days - 13 Nights | Discover Buddhist Foot Prints in Pakistan

Departure : Group Trips - | Private depending on bookings

Fitness Level 2: 6-7 hours of physical activity per day | 1500-15,535 ft above sea level.

Culture and Heritage

$2500-4000/person

Introduction

Join us at Crossroads Adventure on a 14-day journey through Northern Pakistan, exploring its rich Buddhist heritage. Discover ancient relics, stupas, and monasteries from the Gandhara civilization in Taxila and Swat, admire the Kargah Buddha in Gilgit, and uncover the historical significance of Buddhist rock carvings along the Karakoram Highway. This Experience offers a unique glimpse into Pakistan's vibrant cultural and historical landscape.

Highlights

Historical and Cultural Odyssey

Venture on a journey through time as you explore Taxila's UNESCO World Heritage Sites, delve into Peshawar's vibrant cultural scene, and uncover the ancient Buddhist remains of Takht Bhai and Swat Valley.

Legendary Karakoram Highway Expedition

Experience the thrill of traversing the iconic Karakoram Highway, pausing to admire Buddhist inscriptions and awe-inspiring mountain vistas on your way to Gilgit.

Enchanting Hunza Valley Discovery

Immerse yourself in the enchanting beauty of Hunza Valley, where you'll visit iconic landmarks like Baltit Fort, Attabad Lake, and picturesque villages such as Gulmit and Ghulkin.

Spectacular Natural Wonders

Marvel at the breathtaking landscapes of Passu, including the iconic Passu Cones and the majestic Batura Glacier, while soaking in the tranquility of Hussaini and Passu villages.

Culinary and Cultural Immersion

Indulge in the rich flavors of traditional Hunza cuisine and immerse yourself in the authentic village life of Hussaini and Passu, interacting with locals and gaining insight into their unique cultural heritage.

Itinerary

Upon your arrival in Islamabad, you'll be welcomed and assisted to your accommodation to settle in and unwind from your journey.

Depart from Islamabad to Taxila, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its archaeological sites dating back to the Gandhara civilization. Explore the ancient ruins and Buddhist relics, including stupas and monasteries, before heading to Peshawar. Upon arrival, Venture on a city Experience, discovering the rich cultural and historical significance of this vibrant city.

Visit the Peshawar Museum, known for its extensive collection of Gandharan art, including sculptures, coins, and artifacts from ancient Buddhist civilizations. Afterward, immerse yourself in the bustling streets of Peshawar, exploring local markets and landmarks that showcase the city's unique charm.

Depart from Peshawar to Takht Bhai, home to the impressive Takht Bhai Buddhist Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the ancient ruins of this important monastic complex before continuing your journey to the picturesque Swat Valley, known as the "Switzerland of the East."

Discover the rich Buddhist heritage of Swat as you explore ancient stupas, monasteries, and rock carvings scattered throughout the valley. Delve into the history and significance of these archaeological sites, offering insights into the region's Buddhist past.

Journey through the scenic landscapes of Swat Valley, passing through the mountainous terrain to reach Shangla. Enjoy the breathtaking views before continuing to Bisham, a town situated along the banks of the mighty Indus River.

Venture on an unforgettable journey along the legendary Karakoram Highway, one of the world's highest paved roads. Along the way, make stops to admire Buddhist inscriptions and rock carvings before arriving in Gilgit, the capital of Gilgit-Baltistan.

Explore Gilgit's cultural landmarks, including the ancient Kargah Buddha, a significant Buddhist rock carving dating back to the 7th century, overlooking the city. Then, Adventure to Hunza Valley, renowned for its breathtaking scenery and vibrant culture.

Spend the day exploring the enchanting Hunza Valley, visiting iconic landmarks such as Baltit Fort and Altit Fort, which were historically influenced by Buddhist culture. Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding peaks, including Rakaposhi and Ultar Sar.

Venture on a scenic drive to Attabad Lake, a mesmerizing turquoise gem formed by a landslide. Continue to Gulmit, home to ancient Buddhist petroglyphs, and Ghulkin, charming villages offering opportunities for cultural immersion and exploration.

Visit Passu, known for its stunning views of the Passu Cones and the Batura Glacier. Explore the ancient Buddhist rock carvings in the area. Afterward, return to Gilgit, soaking in the beauty of the Karakoram Range along the way.

Depart from Gilgit and journey back to Bisham, enjoying the picturesque landscapes of Gilgit-Baltistan one last time before concluding your adventure.

Drive back to Islamabad, where you'll bid farewell to the mountains and valleys of northern Pakistan. Rest and relax in Islamabad before your departure.

Transfer to the Islamabad International Airport for your onward journey, taking with you cherished memories of your expedition through Pakistan's northern wonders and its rich Buddhist heritage.

Who can join us?

The Buddhist Heritage Tour in Pakistan is ideal for history enthusiasts, cultural explorers, and those seeking a unique journey through the heart of Buddhist history in South Asia. Designed for travelers with a passion for archaeology and spirituality, this tour combines exploration of UNESCO World Heritage Sites, serene valleys, and ancient monasteries. Participants will enjoy scenic drives, cultural immersion in local communities, and guided tours of some of the most significant Buddhist sites in Pakistan. Whether you're a scholar of Buddhist heritage or a curious traveler, this tour offers a deep dive into the rich cultural and historical legacy of Buddhism in the region.
